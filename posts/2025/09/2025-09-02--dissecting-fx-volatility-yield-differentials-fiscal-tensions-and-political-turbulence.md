The EURUSD cross has regained momentum in recent weeks, but the drivers beneath the surface are shifting. What was once a straightforward rates-spread story has become increasingly complicated by fiscal dynamics in Germany and political turbulence in France. At the same time, the market’s focus on the US short end means that relative yield differentials remain the anchor point for direction.

In the sections below, I break down how these forces are interacting, what the charts are telling us, why the usual relationships don’t always hold, and how political and policy developments are shaping the outlook for EURUSD.
